# ASCENDIA Product Marketing Context

This context is based on the existing ASCENDIA website, `brand-dna.md`, `SKILL.md`, and `AGENTS.md`. It is intended to guide future website copy and messaging work without changing the site yet.

## 1. Target Audience

ASCENDIA primarily serves Chinese-speaking international students and recent graduates who are studying abroad or have overseas education backgrounds. They are targeting competitive professional tracks such as consulting, finance, business analytics, marketing, tech-adjacent business roles, and broader corporate positions.

The core student profile:

- Undergraduate juniors and seniors preparing for internships or full-time recruiting.
- Master's students with a short recruiting timeline and high pressure to focus quickly.
- Recent graduates within 0-2 years who need to reset their job-search strategy.
- Students with decent academic or project experience, but unclear positioning and weak job-market storytelling.

Secondary audience:

- Parents who influence or fund the decision and need to feel the service is credible, premium, and not anxiety-driven.
- Potential mentors or collaborators who need to understand ASCENDIA's standards and service philosophy.

The user often wants practical help, but also reassurance. They need to feel: "This team understands my situation, can see what is blocking me, and will help me move with a clear next step."

## 2. Core Pain Points

- Direction feels blurry. Students do not know which industries, roles, and application sequence make sense for their background.
- Their experience is not being translated well. Projects, internships, coursework, and campus work exist, but they do not read like role-relevant evidence.
- Interview answers feel scattered. Without a Storybank, strong experiences become loose examples rather than structured answers.
- Networking is hard to start and harder to sustain. Students are unsure how to write, follow up, ask useful questions, and turn conversations into referrals or real insight.
- Application work feels fragmented. Resume edits, interview prep, outreach, and deadlines happen separately, so progress is hard to judge.
- Feedback loops are weak. After a call or revision, students may still be unsure what to do first, what good looks like, and when to adjust.
- Trust is fragile. Students and parents are alert to overpromising, cheap resume templates, and sales-heavy career services.

## 3. ASCENDIA's Positioning

ASCENDIA is a premium boutique career studio for international students. It helps students turn vague career anxiety into a clear, executable job-search path through structured mentorship, role-specific preparation, and consistent execution support.

Recommended positioning line:

ASCENDIA 帮留学生把模糊的职业焦虑，拆成看得见、做得到、能复盘的求职路径。

Expanded Chinese positioning:

ASCENDIA 是面向留学生的高端职业规划与求职陪跑工作室。我们从定位、简历与 LinkedIn、Storybank、Mock Interview 到 Networking，帮助学生把经历讲成目标岗位能读懂的能力证据，并在每个阶段知道下一步该怎么推进。

English positioning:

ASCENDIA is a boutique career studio for international students, combining structured career planning, role-specific mentorship, and hands-on job-search execution.

## 4. Differentiation

- 2v1 mentor matrix. A lead mentor owns long-term direction, application rhythm, and next steps; a target-role mentor brings industry depth, interview realism, and networking context.
- Path first, service second. The service is organized around Diagnose, Position, Rebuild, Practice, Connect, and Convert, so students see a sequence rather than a menu.
- Real workflow assets. The brand should show concrete deliverables: resume and LinkedIn revisions, Storybank structure, mock interview feedback, networking messages, next-step notes, and progress tracking.
- Premium with warmth. The brand can feel high-end and consulting-adjacent, but the message should stay human, specific, and mentor-like.
- Results shown with restraint. Numbers and cases are used to build trust, not to create pressure or imply guaranteed outcomes.
- Role-fit orientation. ASCENDIA emphasizes matching background, target role, expression, and outreach strategy, instead of encouraging blind applications.
- Anti-template stance. The service should never feel like low-cost resume editing, generic career coaching, or influencer-style job-search content.

## 5. Tone Of Voice

The voice should sound like an experienced mentor: clear judgment, calm confidence, and practical warmth.

Voice principles:

- Premium but sincere: polished language, no fake grandeur.
- Sharp but not aggressive: name the problem directly, then give a grounded path forward.
- Specific before inspirational: talk about resume evidence, Storybank, interview follow-up, outreach rhythm, and next steps.
- Natural Chinese: concise, conversational, and suitable for Xiaohongshu-style readers without becoming cute or slang-heavy.
- Mentor-like: use "你" when useful, but avoid lecturing.

Good sentence patterns:

- 你已经做了很多准备。现在更重要的是判断：方向、材料、面试和 networking，哪个最影响结果。
- 一段经历能不能被看见，关键在于它有没有被翻译成目标岗位听得懂的证据。
- 求职的关键，是把每一步放到正确的顺序里，少做无效消耗。
- 先看路径，再谈动作。这样每一次修改和练习都更有意义。
- 每次推进后，学生都应该知道下一步做什么、为什么做、怎么判断有效。

Avoid:

- Repetitive "不是……而是……" structures.
- Generic consulting jargon such as "赋能", "抓手", "生态", "闭环" when plain wording is clearer.
- Anxiety phrases such as "不报就晚了", "逆天改命", "碾压同龄人", "全网最强", "保 offer", "100% 拿 offer".
- Overly sales-like copy, vague slogans, and inflated transformation claims.

## 6. Proof Points

Documented proof points in the current website and brand files:

- 200+ successful coaching cases.
- 92% success rate.
- 180% average offer rate.
- 2v1 mentor matrix.
- First-call deliverables within 3 days.
- Response within 3 working hours.
- Next Step confirmed after each meeting.
- Progress warning and tracking system.
- Case formats showing background, problem, strategy, and result.

Important publishing note:

Before final website copy goes live, define and verify each metric. For example, clarify what counts as "success rate", how "average offer rate" is calculated, what period the data covers, and which student cohorts are included. The tone should frame these as evidence of a tested process, not as guarantees.

## 7. Core Service Offerings

- Free 1v1 career diagnosis: review school, year, background, target direction, current blockers, and immediate priorities.
- Career positioning and target path design: clarify industry, role, application order, and realistic tradeoffs.
- Resume and LinkedIn rebuild: translate experience into role-relevant evidence and align offline and online professional narratives.
- Storybank development: organize project, leadership, conflict, impact, and business-judgment stories for interviews.
- Mock Interview training: practice behavioral, case, technical-adjacent, and role-specific questions with direct feedback.
- Networking strategy and execution: design outreach, follow-up, coffee chat questions, referral timing, and post-conversation actions.
- Application rhythm and progress tracking: set priorities, review feedback, track risks, and keep weekly actions visible.
- 2v1 mentor support: combine long-term planning with target-role depth so advice stays both strategic and practical.

## 8. Website Conversion Goal

Primary conversion goal:

Get qualified students to submit or book a free 1v1 career diagnosis with enough context for ASCENDIA to assess fit: school, year, target direction, and current blocker.

Secondary conversion goals:

- Help students understand ASCENDIA's service path within the first 10 seconds.
- Build parent trust through restraint, structure, proof points, and clear service commitments.
- Make the 2v1 mentor matrix feel concrete and valuable.
- Show that ASCENDIA can diagnose the student's real bottleneck before recommending a service.

Recommended CTA framing:

- 预约 1v1 职业诊断
- 获取下一步求职优先级
- 让导师先看你的求职卡点

The website should move users from "I feel stuck" to "I know what information to submit, and I trust ASCENDIA to help me see the next step."
