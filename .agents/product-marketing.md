*Last updated: 2026-06-06*

This document is the canonical ASCENDIA product marketing context for local marketing skills. It is based on the existing website file `index.html`, brand files `brand-dna.md`, `SKILL.md`, `AGENTS.md`, and the local skills in `.agents/skills`.

## Product Overview

**One-liner:**
ASCENDIA is a premium boutique career studio helping international students turn unclear job-search anxiety into a structured, executable career path.

**What it does:**
ASCENDIA supports international students from career positioning through resume and LinkedIn rebuild, Storybank development, mock interviews, networking execution, and offer-oriented progress tracking. The work is structured around mentor guidance, role-specific preparation, and concrete next steps rather than generic advice.

**Product category:**
International student career planning, premium job-search coaching, boutique career consulting, career studio, overseas student career services.

**Product type:**
High-touch professional service / boutique consulting studio.

**Business model:**
Paid career consulting and job-search support, likely beginning with a free 1v1 diagnosis. Specific package structure and pricing are not defined in the current website files.

## Target Audience

**Target customers:**
Chinese-speaking international students and recent graduates with overseas education backgrounds who are preparing for competitive professional recruiting.

**Primary segments:**
- Undergraduate juniors and seniors preparing for internships or full-time recruiting.
- Master's students with short recruiting timelines and high pressure to focus quickly.
- Recent graduates within 0-2 years who need to reset their job-search strategy.
- Students targeting consulting, finance, business analytics, marketing, tech-adjacent business roles, and broader corporate positions.

**Secondary stakeholders:**
- Parents who influence or fund the decision and need credibility, restraint, and visible structure.
- Potential mentors or collaborators who need to understand ASCENDIA's standards and service philosophy.

**Decision-makers:**
The student is usually the user and emotional decision-maker. Parents may be financial buyers. For high-ticket services, both need to feel the service is premium, sincere, practical, and not anxiety-driven.

**Primary use case:**
A student feels stuck in the job search and needs a clear diagnosis of what is blocking progress: positioning, resume/LinkedIn expression, interview storytelling, networking rhythm, or application execution.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Help me understand which career path and application sequence fit my background.
- Help me translate my experience into evidence that target roles can understand.
- Help me prepare interviews and networking in a way that feels structured and realistic.
- Help me know what to do next each week instead of guessing.

**Use cases:**
- A master's student has limited time before recruitment deadlines and needs priorities fast.
- A student has several experiences, but the resume reads scattered or too academic.
- A student can answer interview questions, but the stories lack structure, business judgment, or impact.
- A student knows networking matters, but cannot start, follow up, or turn conversations into useful insight.
- A family wants premium support but is cautious about exaggerated career-service claims.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:**
International students are often working hard, but their job search lacks a clear operating system. Direction, materials, interviews, networking, and follow-up happen separately, so effort does not reliably translate into opportunity.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Traditional study-abroad agencies may not have enough role-specific career depth.
- Low-cost resume editing can polish wording without fixing positioning or evidence.
- Generic career courses often teach concepts but do not adapt to the student's timeline and background.
- School career centers may be useful, but can be too broad or too light-touch for competitive recruiting.
- Self-serve AI tools can draft language, but cannot judge fit, sequence, tradeoffs, or live interview nuance.

**What it costs them:**
Missed deadlines, weaker interviews, lower response rates, unfocused applications, reduced confidence, and avoidable stress during a time-sensitive recruiting window.

**Emotional tension:**
Students feel they "should already know what to do" because they are studying abroad and have invested heavily in education. Parents want to help, but are wary of services that promise too much. The emotional need is relief through clarity, not pressure through fear.

**Core pain points:**
- Direction feels blurry.
- Experience is not translated into role-relevant evidence.
- Interview stories feel scattered.
- Networking feels awkward, unclear, or hard to sustain.
- Application rhythm is fragmented.
- Feedback loops are weak.
- Trust is fragile because the category contains overpromising and template-based services.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:**
Premium career consulting studios, overseas student career coaching services, and high-touch job-search mentor programs. They may fall short if their work feels sales-heavy, template-based, or disconnected from concrete weekly execution.

**Secondary competitors:**
University career centers, alumni mentors, one-off resume editors, mock interview coaches, and online career courses. These can help with isolated tasks, but may not connect positioning, materials, interviews, networking, and progress tracking into one path.

**Indirect competitors:**
Self-study, peer advice, Xiaohongshu/LinkedIn career content, AI writing tools, and generic resume templates. These lower the cost of starting, but can leave students with too many opinions and not enough judgment.

**Anti-positioning:**
ASCENDIA should not sound like a traditional study-abroad agency, low-cost resume editing shop, influencer job-search account, anxiety-driven training class, or generic AI landing page.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- 2v1 mentor matrix: lead mentor plus target-role mentor.
- Structured path: Diagnose, Position, Rebuild, Practice, Connect, Convert.
- Concrete workflow assets: resume/LinkedIn revisions, Storybank, mock feedback, networking message examples, next-step notes, progress tracking.
- Premium but human tone: polished, credible, and warm without sounding distant.
- Restrained proof: metrics and cases support the process without implying guaranteed outcomes.
- Role-fit orientation: match background, target role, expression, and outreach strategy instead of encouraging blind applications.

**How we do it differently:**
ASCENDIA starts by diagnosing the student's real bottleneck, then sequences the work. The service does not present a flat menu of tasks; it creates a path where each action has a reason, owner, and next step.

**Why that's better:**
Students get less noise and more judgment. They can see which action matters now, how to execute it, and how to evaluate whether it is working.

**Why customers choose ASCENDIA:**
They want premium support that feels both strategic and practical. They do not just want someone to edit wording; they want someone experienced to look at the whole job-search system and help them move with focus.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Is this just expensive resume editing?" | No. Resume and LinkedIn work are part of a broader path that includes positioning, Storybank, mock interviews, networking, and next-step tracking. |
| "Can you guarantee an offer?" | ASCENDIA should never promise guaranteed offers. The honest promise is a clearer path, stronger expression, better preparation, and more disciplined execution. |
| "Will this fit my background?" | The first 1v1 diagnosis should identify the student's school, year, target direction, current blocker, and realistic next priority before recommending a path. |
| "I can use AI or templates myself." | AI can help draft, but students still need judgment: what to target, which story to use, how to frame impact, and when to adjust strategy. |
| "Will it create more pressure?" | The tone and process should reduce pressure by making priorities visible and manageable. |

**Anti-persona:**
Students looking for guaranteed offers, shortcut promises, one-time cheap edits, passive hand-holding with no execution, or "insider trick" style job-search content are not a good fit.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:**
The current approach feels scattered. Students are tired of changing resumes repeatedly, applying without feedback, preparing interviews late, and reading too many conflicting tips.

**Pull:**
ASCENDIA offers a calm, premium, structured path with mentor judgment, role-specific depth, and concrete next steps.

**Habit:**
Students may keep relying on templates, school resources, peer advice, Xiaohongshu posts, or AI drafts because these are familiar and low-friction.

**Anxiety:**
They worry the service may be too expensive, too sales-driven, not customized enough, or unable to understand their specific background.

**Ethical psychology to apply:**
- Reduce activation energy with a simple diagnosis form.
- Use social proof carefully through metrics and case studies.
- Use authority through mentor structure and role-specific expertise.
- Use contrast by showing the difference between scattered effort and sequenced execution.
- Avoid manipulative scarcity, fear-based urgency, and exaggerated loss framing.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:**
Premium but sincere. Sharp but not aggressive. Professional, calm, credible, and mentor-like.

**Style:**
Use natural Chinese that sounds clear and human. It can be Xiaohongshu-friendly in rhythm, but should avoid cute internet slang, empty inspiration, and exaggerated claims. English can appear in service terms and brand phrases, but should not make the copy feel cold.

**Personality:**
Structured, warm, discerning, international, restrained, practical.

**Copy principles from local skills:**
- Clarity over cleverness.
- Customer language over company language.
- One idea per section.
- Specificity over vague claims.
- Honest proof over inflated persuasion.
- Reduce risk near CTAs by setting clear expectations.

**Good Chinese line examples:**
- 你已经做了很多准备。现在更重要的是判断：方向、材料、面试和 networking，哪个最影响结果。
- 一段经历能不能被看见，关键在于它有没有被翻译成目标岗位听得懂的证据。
- 求职的关键，是把每一步放到正确的顺序里，少做无效消耗。
- 先看路径，再谈动作。这样每一次修改和练习都更有意义。
- 每次推进后，学生都应该知道下一步做什么、为什么做、怎么判断有效。

## Core Service Offerings

- Free 1v1 career diagnosis: review school, year, target direction, current blockers, and immediate priorities.
